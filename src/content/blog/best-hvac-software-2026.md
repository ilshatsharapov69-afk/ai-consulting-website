## The Four Contenders at a Glance

| Platform | Monthly Cost | Best For | Minimum Team Size |
|---|---|---|---|
| ServiceTitan | $3,000-5,000/mo (with implementation) | Established shops, 20+ techs | 15-20 employees |
| Housecall Pro | $49-349/mo | Growing residential teams | 1-15 employees |
| Jobber | $49-249/mo | Small crews, simple workflows | 1-10 employees |
| FieldEdge | Custom pricing (~$100-300/user/mo) | Commercial & mixed-service | 10-30 employees |

Now let's break each one down with real numbers, real strengths, and real problems nobody talks about in the marketing brochures.

## ServiceTitan: The Enterprise Play

**Real pricing:** $3,000-5,000 per month is the range most small-to-mid HVAC shops end up paying. That includes per-user licensing, onboarding fees (often $3,000-8,000 upfront), and the various add-on modules you will inevitably need. Some larger operations pay north of $7,000/month. They do not publish pricing on their website for a reason.

**Best for:** Established HVAC companies with 20+ technicians, dedicated office staff, and annual revenue above $3M. Companies that want deep reporting, marketing ROI tracking, and a fully integrated platform.

### Pros
- **Most complete feature set** in the industry: dispatching, pricebook management, memberships, marketing scorecards, financing integrations, and reporting dashboards that actually tell you something useful
- **Marketing ROI tracking** is genuinely best-in-class. You can see which ad campaigns produce booked jobs, not just calls
- **Pricebook and presentation tools** help techs sell more effectively in the field
- **Large integration ecosystem** with QuickBooks, equipment manufacturers, and financing partners

### Cons
- **The cost is brutal for small shops.** A 10-person company paying $4,000/month is spending $48,000/year before they see a single dollar of ROI
- **Implementation takes 2-4 months.** Your team will be frustrated during the transition, and productivity drops before it rises
- **Long contracts.** Most agreements are 12-36 months. Getting out early is expensive
- **Complexity requires a dedicated admin.** If nobody on your team owns the system, you will use 30% of what you are paying for
- **Customer support quality has declined** as they have scaled. Wait times and ticket resolution are a common complaint in 2026

### Who Should NOT Use ServiceTitan
If you have fewer than 15 employees, you will overpay dramatically. If you do not have at least one person who can manage the platform full-time (or close to it), you will never get the value out of it. And if your annual revenue is under $2M, the math simply does not work.

## Housecall Pro: The Sweet Spot for Growing Teams

**Real pricing:** $49/month for a single user (Basic), $129/month for 1-5 users (Essentials), $349/month for unlimited users (MAX). These are real published prices as of early 2026. Add-ons for features like recurring service agreements or advanced reporting cost extra.

**Best for:** Residential HVAC contractors with 1-15 employees who want a clean, modern interface without enterprise complexity.

### Pros
- **Genuinely easy to learn.** Most techs are comfortable within a week, which is a massive advantage when you are trying to get buy-in from field staff
- **Online booking and customer notifications** work well out of the box. Customers can book, get text updates, and pay online without you setting up complicated workflows
- **Solid mobile app.** Techs can manage their day, collect payments, and capture photos without constant training
- **Pricing is transparent** and scales reasonably as your team grows
- **QuickBooks integration** is straightforward and reliable

### Cons
- **Reporting is shallow** compared to ServiceTitan or FieldEdge. If you need deep financial analysis or marketing attribution, you will hit walls
- **Inventory management is basic.** If you run a parts warehouse or need real-time stock tracking across trucks, this is not the platform
- **Commercial job management is weak.** Multi-day jobs, complex bidding, and project tracking are not its strength
- **The jump from Essentials to MAX is steep** if you mainly need one or two features from the higher tier

### Who Should NOT Use Housecall Pro
If you do significant commercial work, Housecall Pro will frustrate you. If you need advanced inventory tracking or detailed job costing, look elsewhere. And if you have more than 20 techs, you may outgrow it faster than you expect.

## Jobber: Simple, Affordable, Gets Out of Your Way

**Real pricing:** $49/month (Core, 1 user), $129/month (Connect, up to 5 users), $249/month (Grow, up to 15 users). Straightforward pricing, no hidden implementation fees, no long-term contracts required.

**Best for:** Small HVAC crews of 1-10 people who want scheduling, invoicing, and customer communication without the bloat.

### Pros
- **Fastest setup of any platform on this list.** You can be running jobs through Jobber within a day
- **Client hub** lets customers approve quotes, pay invoices, and request work online. It is clean and professional
- **Quoting and invoicing workflow** is simple and effective. For straightforward residential service calls, it covers everything you need
- **Excellent customer support.** Consistently rated highest among these four for responsiveness and helpfulness
- **Affordable enough** that it pays for itself if it saves you even a few hours per month on admin

### Cons
- **Not built for complexity.** Multi-phase jobs, detailed labor tracking, and advanced dispatching logic are limited
- **No pricebook management.** You cannot build and manage flat-rate pricing the way you can in ServiceTitan or FieldEdge
- **Limited reporting and analytics.** You get the basics but nothing that helps with strategic decisions
- **Marketing tools are minimal.** No call tracking, no campaign attribution, no membership management

### Who Should NOT Use Jobber
If you are running a $2M+ operation with 15+ techs, Jobber will start to feel like a toy. If you rely on flat-rate pricing and need a pricebook, it is the wrong tool. And if you need serious reporting for business decisions, you will end up exporting to spreadsheets anyway.

## FieldEdge: The Underrated Commercial Option

**Real pricing:** FieldEdge does not publish pricing. Based on what contractors report, expect $100-300 per user per month depending on your package and team size. Implementation and training fees are separate and vary. Total cost for a 15-person team is typically $2,000-4,000/month.

**Best for:** HVAC companies that do a mix of commercial and residential work with 10-30 employees, especially those who want strong QuickBooks integration and equipment tracking.

### Pros
- **Best QuickBooks integration** of any platform in this space. If your accountant lives in QuickBooks, FieldEdge will make them happy
- **Equipment tracking and service history** is genuinely useful for commercial maintenance contracts
- **Flat-rate pricebook** is solid and helps standardize pricing across your team
- **Dispatching board** is visual and intuitive, which dispatchers appreciate
- **Good fit for mixed residential/commercial** operations that need to handle both workflows

### Cons
- **The interface feels dated** compared to Housecall Pro or Jobber. It works, but it is not winning any design awards
- **Mobile app has historically been inconsistent.** It has improved in 2026 but still gets complaints about speed and reliability
- **Pricing opacity** makes it hard to budget. You have to go through a sales process to get a number
- **Fewer modern integrations** than ServiceTitan. If you want to connect marketing tools, financing platforms, or newer scheduling apps, options are limited
- **Smaller user community** means fewer online resources, tutorials, and peer support

### Who Should NOT Use FieldEdge
If you are a purely residential shop under 10 employees, FieldEdge is overkill and overpriced. If you care about modern UI and a polished tech experience, your team will push back. And if you need advanced marketing analytics, this is not where you will find them.

## Which One Should YOU Pick? A Decision Tree

Choosing HVAC software does not have to be overwhelming. Walk through these questions:

**1. How many employees do you have?**
- **1-5 employees:** Start with **Jobber**. It is affordable, simple, and you can switch later if you outgrow it.
- **5-15 employees:** **Housecall Pro** is probably your sweet spot. Enough features to grow, not enough complexity to drown in.
- **15-30 employees:** Evaluate **FieldEdge** (especially if you do commercial work) or **ServiceTitan** (if you are primarily residential and want marketing tools).
- **30+ employees:** **ServiceTitan** is likely worth the investment at this scale.

**2. Do you do commercial work?**
- **Mostly commercial or mixed:** **FieldEdge** handles this better than the others.
- **Purely residential:** **Housecall Pro** or **ServiceTitan** depending on your size.

**3. What is your biggest pain point right now?**
- **Scheduling and dispatching chaos:** Any of these will help, but **Housecall Pro** and **Jobber** get you there fastest.
- **Technicians not selling enough:** **ServiceTitan** pricebook and presentation tools are built for this.
- **Invoicing and payment collection:** **Jobber** makes this dead simple.
- **Tracking equipment and service agreements:** **FieldEdge** was designed around this.

**4. What is your budget reality?**
- **Under $200/month:** **Jobber** or **Housecall Pro** Basic.
- **$200-500/month:** **Housecall Pro** MAX or **FieldEdge** for small teams.
- **$2,000+/month:** **ServiceTitan** or **FieldEdge** at scale.

The stats tell the story:

- **Most contractors use 5-8 different apps that do not talk to each other.** They have one tool for scheduling, another for invoicing, a spreadsheet for estimating, a shared drive for photos, and sticky notes for follow-ups. Adding another app to this mess does not help.

## Hidden Costs Nobody Mentions

Before you commit to any platform, budget for these:

- **Training time.** Every hour a tech spends learning software is an hour they are not running calls. For ServiceTitan, expect 20-40 hours per person. For Jobber, maybe 4-6 hours.
- **Data migration.** Moving your customer database, job history, and pricing from your current system is never free. Even if the vendor says it is included, your team will spend significant time cleaning and verifying data.
- **Integration costs.** Connecting your field service software to QuickBooks, your phone system, and your marketing tools often requires third-party tools like Zapier ($20-70/month) or custom development.
- **Opportunity cost of switching.** The productivity dip during transition is real. Plan for a 15-25% drop in efficiency for the first month.
