# Case Study Consent — Plain English

This is a plain-English consent form. It exists so that when a client asks "can you write this up?" — or when you want to ask them — both sides know exactly what's being agreed to before anything is written.

**Who uses this:** Ilshat (you), when you want to publish a case study, and the client (them), when they're agreeing to it.

**When to present it:** after the audit is delivered and the client has seen their report — not before. Don't ask for consent on work that doesn't exist yet.

**Format:** can be sent as-is (copy into a Google Doc, Notion page, or DocuSign), or adapted into a short email with the key points.

---

## CASE STUDY CONSENT — [CLIENT NAME OR "CLIENT"]

### 1. What this is

You (`[CLIENT]`) are agreeing that I, Ilshat Sharapov of Setpoint Audit, may publish a case study describing the work I did for you under our Data-to-Dollars Audit engagement completed on `[DATE]`. The case study will appear on https://setpointaudit.com and may be referenced in marketing materials, social posts, or a YouTube video.

### 2. What the case study will include

- A description of your business at a general level (industry, revenue band, employee count range)
- The systems your data came from (e.g., QuickBooks, HubSpot, Toast)
- What the audit found — quantified revenue leaks with dollar amounts
- What actions you took in the 30/60/90 days after delivery, if you choose to share that
- Any follow-up data about measured results, if you choose to share that

### 3. Anonymization options — pick one

- [ ] **Option A — Named.** Your business name, the owner's name, and city may appear in the case study.
- [ ] **Option B — Named business, anonymous owner.** Your business name appears; no owner first name or quotes.
- [ ] **Option C — Anonymized industry only.** The case study refers to you only as "a [industry] business in [region]." No business name, no owner name. Revenue is quoted in a range ($X–$Y million), not exact.
- [ ] **Option D — Fully anonymous.** Industry descriptor only ("a professional services business"), no region, no names, revenue in wide bucket. The audit findings are the subject; you are not identifiable.

### 4. What stays private — regardless of option

Even in Option A (fully named), I will NOT publish:

- Exact revenue figures (always expressed in ranges or buckets)
- Customer names, customer lists, or customer revenue breakdowns
- Employee names (other than you, if Option A)
- Tenant IDs, API keys, database IDs, or anything technical that could identify systems
- Screenshots from your actual software unless they're blurred of any PII
- Any data you flag on the review call (Section 6) as private

### 5. What I will publish — in all cases

- The audit methodology steps (universal)
- The patterns matched (the six recurring leak types)
- The aggregate dollar impact of findings
- The structural shape of the leaks (e.g., "unbilled invoices >45 days old")
- My attribution of any finding back to the universal data types (QuickBooks, CRM, etc.)

### 6. You review before publish

I will share the full draft with you at least 5 business days before publishing. You have veto power on:

- Specific quotes attributed to you (you can approve, edit, or remove any quote)
- Specific dollar numbers (you can ask me to round, bucket, or remove any specific figure)
- Any claim about your business you feel is inaccurate

After your review and approval, I publish. If we can't reach approval, I don't publish the case study — no case study is better than one you're not comfortable with.

### 7. Withdrawal

If at any time after publication — 30 days later, 3 years later — you change your mind and want the case study taken down, say so and I will unpublish it within 72 hours. No reason required. No penalty, no clawback of the refund or fee.

### 8. No revised claims after publishing

Once published with your approval, I won't edit it to say anything stronger than what was there on publish day. I may edit to correct errors, add links, or update outdated context. Any material change gets a fresh approval cycle.

### 9. No compensation either direction

You don't owe me anything for appearing in a case study. I don't owe you anything for agreeing to appear (no discount, no free follow-up work, no referral fee).

### 10. Jurisdiction / law

This is a non-exclusive consent memorialized in plain English. It's not a contract with mutual obligations beyond what's written above. If you prefer a formal contract, we can translate the same terms into one — ask and I'll draft it.

## Quick-use email version

If a formal form feels heavy for your client, the essentials fit in a 10-sentence email. Use this when the client is already comfortable and just wants to give a quick yes.

> Subject: Case study write-up — quick consent
>
> Hi [name],
>
> Following up on the audit I delivered on [date]. I'd like to write it up as a case study for setpointaudit.com — it would help other business owners in your industry see what the audit actually finds.
>
> Four options for how you appear:
> **A.** Named — business, your first name, city.
> **B.** Named business, no owner name or quote.
> **C.** Anonymous — "a [industry] business in [region]," revenue in a range.
> **D.** Fully anonymous — industry descriptor only.
>
> Whichever you pick, these stay private: customer names, exact revenue, system IDs, anything sensitive I saw in your data.
>
> You'll see the full draft before I publish, with full veto on any quote or number. If you ever change your mind after publish, I take it down within 72 hours, no questions.
>
> Reply with A/B/C/D and I'll draft. Takes about two weeks from your yes to publish.
>
> Thanks,
> Ilshat
